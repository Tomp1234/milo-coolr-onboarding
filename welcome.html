03 / Getting the Best from Milo
How to ask Milo a question.

Milo understands plain English, so there's no special syntax. But a little specificity goes a long way. Here's how to get the sharpest answers.

1.
Be specific with time and scope
"What was our revenue last month vs budget?" gets a sharper answer than "how are we doing?". The more context you give, the more precise Milo's answer.
2.
Add a dimension if you want a breakdown
Drop in "by department", "by client" or "by team" to slice the data however you need it. Milo will restructure the answer accordingly.
3.
Ask 'why' when something looks off
If the numbers surprise you, just ask. "Why did utilisation drop in February?" — Milo will dig into the data and give you an explanation, not just a number.
A note on precision: Milo will always tell you when it's confident and when it's making an inference. If it's not sure, it'll say so — and suggest how to refine the question.
04 / Setting Expectations
What Milo is not.

Milo is a powerful tool — but it's worth being clear about what it's designed to do and where the edges are. No surprises.

—
Not a replacement for your finance team's judgment
Milo surfaces data and explains what it means. Your CFO still owns the narrative, the strategy, and the calls that numbers alone can't make.
—
Not a dashboard builder
Milo answers questions — it doesn't build static reports or visualisations. Think of it as a brilliant analyst you can query any time, not a BI tool you have to configure.
—
Not connected to data outside the five sources — yet
Right now, Milo works with HubSpot, Productive, Hibob, Teamtailor and Xero. If you need data from another system in future, we'll scope that as part of the next phase.

Milo is built to grow with you. The POC is the beginning — not the ceiling. As Coolr's needs evolve, so does Milo.
